Return Runner

App Project Developed to Solve Customer Inconvenience when Returning Items

Abbey Ager: User Experience Designer
Timeline: 3 weeks




GOAL:
Our goal is to reduce the hassle of having to return an item you purchased online to a physical location by providing a service that picks it up at your doorstep.

Competitor Analysis

During our user interviews we found that one responder returns about 50% of her online orders. We saw a great need for the app and after working through some branding ideas the team settled on this logo to represent Return Runner.

Final Thoughts

During our testing we found that the “notifications on/off screen” and the “track your return” screen were too simple. We added informational details to the prototype in order to give the user more of a confirmation. Those details were price, location, date, and progress. We also found that users were confused about what was a button and what was a picture and thought the sign up process was too long. Besides a few minor fixes we needed to make, the rest of the app worked pretty well and we are happy with its new iterations. In the future we will need to design another interface for the drivers who will be picking up the packages for Return Runner. We also would need to install a pretty hefty navigation capability so that we are able to map drivers to the correct locations to drop off the returns. All together the design process was challenging and fun and we are happy with the results.
